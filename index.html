Post #003 · September 2026

First & Bowl: From Concept to Revenue-Positive in 90 Days

A sports hospitality concept had the product, the venue, and the market appetite — but no operational architecture to convert interest into repeatable revenue. The root problem was a booking and segmentation gap: inbound demand was being handled manually, with no systematic way to tier customers by spend propensity or recover abandoned booking intents. I designed a structured booking flow, a lightweight customer classification model, and a tiered pricing architecture. The business crossed the revenue-positive threshold in under 90 days. This post shows the exact operational scaffold — pricing model, segmentation logic, and funnel mechanics — with the actual numbers.

Root Cause Before Remediation

The patch is never the deliverable. Every case study here traces a failure to its structural origin before any solution is proposed. Fixes without root cause analysis are technical debt with a timestamp.

🔧

Vanilla Engineering Discipline

All tools and automation on this site run in your browser with zero external dependencies — no frameworks, no API calls, no vendor runtime. If a system can't be explained without a vendor diagram, it's too fragile to trust.

📐

Evidence, Not Assertion

Claims in these posts are anchored to specific outcomes: error rates, timeline deltas, and revenue figures from documented scenarios. Operational authority without traceable evidence is just marketing.

If Something Here Resonates

These case studies are intentionally detailed. If the methodology connects with a problem you're working through — a broken workflow, an integrity gap, a revenue system that isn't converting — I'm worth a conversation. Or explore the open-source architecture directly and take what's useful.
