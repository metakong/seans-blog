The Framework

DSIE, explained like a neighbor would

DSIE stands for Diagnose, Strategize, Integrate, Execute — the anti-framework behind The DSIE Codex. Four steps, no whiteboard required. It works on broken pipelines, and it works just as well on broken Tuesdays.

D

Step One · Diagnose

Listen first.

Before anything gets "optimized," we figure out what actually hurts — not the symptom you've learned to live with, but the root cause underneath it. Like a good doctor: no prescriptions before the exam.

S

Step Two · Strategize

Make a plan you can read.

A plan is only useful if the person who has to live with it understands it. That means plain-English options, honest trade-offs, and real prices — never a 40-slide deck.

I

Step Three · Integrate

New tools, no whiplash.

Technology should fit around your people, not the other way around. Nothing that works today gets ripped out. Your team keeps making the decisions — the machines just take the busywork.

E

Step Four · Execute

Do the work — not a report about the work.

This is where most advice-givers hand you a binder and a bill. DSIE ends with a running system — built, tested, and operated — so you're paying for action, not advice.
